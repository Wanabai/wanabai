# Trademark Policy

**"Wanabai"** and associated marks are names reserved for the future non-profit foundation that will steward this project. The full policy this document anticipates is specified in the *License and Governance* section of [WHITEPAPER.md](./WHITEPAPER.md), under *Foundation stewardship*, *Trademark and certification*, and *Foundation selection*.

## Current state

The foundation has not yet been formed. Until it is, the marks are held in trust by the project maintainer, Leonardo Saturnino, on behalf of the future foundation. The maintainer does not grant commercial licenses to the marks. This document will be superseded by the foundation's published policy once the foundation is established.

## Current usage rules

Until the foundation publishes its own policy, the following interim rules apply:

1. **Unmodified use.** Any party may run the unmodified software in this repository and refer to it by the name "Wanabai."
2. **Modified forks.** Modified versions must use a different name. You may describe a fork as "based on Wanabai" or "a fork of Wanabai," but you may not call the modified work "Wanabai" without written permission.
3. **Certification claims prohibited.** No party may claim their implementation is "certified Wanabai" or equivalent. No certification program exists yet; one will be administered by the foundation once it is formed. Claiming certification in its absence is prohibited.
4. **Domain and organization names.** The canonical references for the project are the domain `wanabai.com` and the GitHub organization `github.com/wanabai`. Do not register domains, GitHub organizations, or other namespace identifiers that imply official project affiliation.
5. **Trade dress.** Do not use project logos, color schemes, or visual identifiers in a way that implies official endorsement of a third-party work.

## What this policy is not

This is not a legal grant and does not create a trademark license. It is the maintainer's stated intent to hold the marks in trust for the future foundation and to prevent commercial capture of the name before that foundation exists. The Apache License 2.0 under which the code is released covers the code only; it does not grant any rights to the project name or marks (see Section 6 of the Apache License).
